Introduction¶

Gun safety is of valuable concern to the United States security. For the reason of personal security/hunting purposes, it is possible for individual to own guns. To keep a track of the number of buyers, and also to be informed of the backkground of the prospective client is of utmost importance for gun dealers.

Hence to keep track of this, FBI's National Instant Criminal Background Check System (NICS) has maintained a database of the background of buyers. The NICS is hence used by dealers to determine whether a prospective buyer is eligible to buy firearms or explosives. Gun shops call into this system to ensure that each customer does not have a criminal record or isn’t otherwise ineligible to make a purchase. Further, there is also a record on the number of buyers across states within the country.

In this project, we will use the dataset (firearms checks made by NICS) to determine and analyse trends in the firearms buyers in the US with respect to the states, type of gun/explosives bought, checks made etc-.

Conclusions¶

In this project we have analysed the FBI gun data from the data collected by the NCIS.

We noticed the following:
1. The gun puchasing has risen continuously on an average since 1998 until 2017 while encountering fluctuations within certain months every year.
2. With respect to the number of handguns, Texas, Florida and California are the highest buyers.
3. With respect to the number of long guns, Texasm California and Pennsylvania are the highest buyers.
4. Unusually, Kentucky is found to be the highest purchaser/renter of guns, though it is not evidently come under the latter two, suggesting the need to look into other parameters in Kentucky which is not within the scope of this study.
5. Finally, the distribution of the total number of guns purchased/rented for the states who are the major gun buyers show a concentrated range for Pennsylvania and a widely distributed area for Kentucky.
